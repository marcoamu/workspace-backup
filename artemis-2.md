## 🗓️ Mission Timeline

### Day 1 - April 1, 2026
- ✅ Launch from Kennedy Space Center (4:30 PM local time)
- ✅ SLS rocket separation
- ✅ Orion solar arrays deployed (63 ft wingspan, 15,000 cells each)
- ✅ Main engine cutoff
- ✅ Perigee Raise Maneuver (PRM)
- ✅ Apogee Raise Burn (ARB)

### Day 2 - April 2, 2026
- ☑️ Crew awakened for Day 2 operations
- ☑️ Post-launch news conference
- ☑️ Orion proximity operations demonstration preparation

### Coming Up
- 🌓 Orbit around the Moon (farthest point: ~4,600 miles beyond dark side)
- 📸 Photo opportunity: Earth and Moon together
- 🏠 Return to Earth

## 🚀 Spacecraft Details

**Orion Spacecraft Name:** Integrity

**Key Stats:**
- Solar array wingspan: ~63 feet
- Solar cells per wing: 15,000
- Power generation: Tracks Sun on 2 axes
- European Service Module

---

## 📝 Notes

- First crewed lunar mission since Apollo 17 (1972)
- Equivalent to Apollo 8 for 21st century
- Crew: 3 Americans + 1 Canadian (Jeremy Hansen, CSA)
